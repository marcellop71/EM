\subsection{Axiom Usage: What's Constructive}

The core definitions (\code{seq}, \code{prod}, \code{aux}) and
their basic properties (\code{seq\_isPrime}, \code{seq\_injective})
are \textbf{fully constructive}: they use only \code{propext} and
\code{Quot.sound} (no \code{Classical.choice}, no
\code{Decidable} instances beyond~$\NN$).  Euclid's theorem
itself (\code{Core/Euclid.lean}) is constructive.

Classical reasoning enters at the reduction level:
\begin{itemize}[nosep]
\item The $\HH \Rightarrow \MC$ proof uses well-founded induction
  (strong induction on $\NN$), which in Lean~4 is constructive but
  relies on \code{Classical.choice} for the
  cofinal-implies-hit argument.
\item Character theory (orthogonality, Fourier inversion) is
  inherently classical via \code{open Classical}.
\item All open hypotheses are stated as \code{def \ldots : Prop},
  never as \code{sorry}'d theorems.  The type-checker guarantees
  that no proof obligation is silently assumed.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{ANT Formalization Status}
\label{sec:ant-formalization}

The sole unformalized input to the master reduction is ANT
(\S\ref{sec:ant-def}): the standard analytic number theory implying
MFRE\@.  The chain is
\[
  \underbrace{\text{Dirichlet} + \text{Sieve}}_{\text{ANT}}
  \;\xrightarrow[\text{\small open}]{\text{W-I}}\;
  \text{WeightedPNTinAP}
  \;\xrightarrow[\text{\small \checkmark}]{\text{strip}}\;
  \text{PrimeLogSum}
  \;\xrightarrow[\text{\small \checkmark}]{\text{Abel}}\;
  \text{PrimesEquidist}
  \;\xrightarrow[\text{\small open}]{\text{Alladi}}\;
  \text{MFRE}
  \;\xrightarrow[\text{\small \checkmark}]{\text{}}\;
  \text{PE}
\]
where ``\checkmark'' denotes machine-verified arrows and
``open'' denotes unformalized steps.
Three files provide the ANT infrastructure:
\code{Population/Tauberian.lean} (557~lines, zero \code{sorry})
proves the Tauberian and stripping steps;
\code{Population/AbelChain.lean} (641~lines, zero \code{sorry}) proves the
full discrete Abel summation chain converting log-weighted prime
sums into reciprocal-weighted sums; and
\code{Population/AlladiDensity.lean} (267~lines, zero \code{sorry})
defines intermediate hypotheses for the Alladi chain and
proves the master reduction
\lean{EM/Population/AlladiDensity.lean}{wpnt\_dsl\_implies\_mc}:
WeightedPNTinAP + Alladi + CRT + DSL~$\Rightarrow$~MC.

\paragraph{Proved steps.}
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{One-sided Tauberian lemma}
(\lean{EM/Population/Tauberian.lean}{one\_sided\_tauberian\_upper}):
for nonnegative $b_n \geq 0$,
$\sum_{n \leq N} b_n \leq N^\varepsilon \cdot \sum_n b_n / n^\varepsilon$.
Elementary proof via $n^\varepsilon \leq N^\varepsilon$.
This gives $O(N^\varepsilon)$ partial-sum bounds from Dirichlet series
behavior, without requiring the full Wiener--Ikehara theorem.

\item \textbf{L-series two-sided bounds}
(\lean{EM/Population/Tauberian.lean}{residueClass\_tsum\_both\_bounds}):
Mathlib's \code{PrimesInAP.lean} proves a \emph{lower} bound on
$\sum \Lambda(n)\mathbf{1}_{n \equiv a}/n^s$ near $s = 1$
via \code{LSeries\_residueClass\_lower\_bound}, but does not export
the corresponding upper bound.  We extract the identity
$\mathrm{tsum} = \mathrm{Aux}(a,s) + (\varphi(q))^{-1}/(s{-}1)$
from Mathlib's proof and derive both bounds.

\item \textbf{Prime power stripping}
(\lean{EM/Population/Tauberian.lean}{prime\_power\_stripping\_proved}):
$\text{WeightedPNTinAP} \Rightarrow \text{PrimeLogSumEquidist}$.
The von Mangoldt function $\Lambda$ counts prime powers
$p^k$ with weight $\log p$; the contribution from $k \geq 2$
is uniformly bounded by $\sum_p 2\log p / p^2 < \infty$.
This 250-line proof strips the higher prime powers and
converts the $\Lambda$-weighted sum into a $(\log p)/p$
sum over primes only.

\item \textbf{Abel summation chain}
(\lean{EM/Population/AbelChain.lean}{prime\_log\_to\_reciprocal\_proved},
641~lines):
  $\text{PrimeLogSumEquidist} \Rightarrow
  \text{PrimesEquidistributedInAP}$.
  Discrete Abel summation (summation by parts) converts the
  log-weighted prime sum $\sum (\log p)/p$ into the
  reciprocal-weighted sum $\sum 1/p$.
  The proof includes derivative identities
  $(\log\log t)' = 1/(t\log t)$ and
  $(-1/\log t)' = 1/(t(\log t)^2)$; integral evaluations via FTC;
  the sandwich bound
  $|\sum_{k=2}^{m-1}(\log(k{+}1) - \log k)/\log(k{+}1)
  - (\log\log m - \log\log 2)| \leq 1/(\log 2)^2$;
  and the discrete Fubini step (double-sum reindexing).

\item \textbf{Full composed chain}
(\lean{EM/Population/AbelChain.lean}{wpnt\_implies\_primes\_equidist\_proved}):
  $\text{WeightedPNTinAP} \Rightarrow
  \text{PrimesEquidistributedInAP}$,
  composing prime power stripping with discrete Abel summation.

\item \textbf{Dirichlet's theorem}
(\lean{EM/Population/Tauberian.lean}{dirichlet\_primes\_in\_ap}):
imported directly from Mathlib's
\code{Nat.infinite\_setOf\_prime\_and\_eq\_mod}.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Remaining gaps.}
Mathlib~v4.29.0 already provides ${\sim}92\%$ of the required
infrastructure: qualitative Dirichlet (via $L$-functions and
non-vanishing on $\operatorname{Re}(s) \geq 1$), Abel summation
(\code{AbelSummation.lean}),
Chebyshev bounds, Dirichlet character orthogonality, and the von
Mangoldt function.  Two gaps remain:

\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Wiener--Ikehara bridge} (sole blocker, ${\sim}300$--$500$~lines):
  convert
  $\sum \Lambda(n)\mathbf{1}_{n \equiv a}/n^{1+\varepsilon}
  = (\varphi(q))^{-1}/\varepsilon + O(1)$
  into the partial-sum asymptotic
  $\sum_{n \leq x} \Lambda(n)\mathbf{1}_{n \equiv a}/n
  = \log x / \varphi(q) + O(1)$.
  Because $\Lambda \geq 0$, a one-sided monotone argument suffices:
  the one-sided Tauberian lemma (proved above) already gives
  $O(N^\varepsilon)$ bounds; what remains is the \emph{asymptotic},
  which requires a quantitative version of the abelian Tauberian
  theorem.  As of March~2026, no external Lean~4 project provides
  a usable Wiener--Ikehara theorem: the PNT+ project
  (Kontorovich--Tao~et~al.)\ includes W-I in its blueprint but
  has not implemented it in Lean, and the \textsc{Gauss} project
  (Math,~Inc.)\ uses a different method (zero-free regions).
  If a W-I formalization lands in Mathlib, the gap closes
  immediately via the L-series identity above.

\item \textbf{Alladi chain} (${\sim}1{,}000$--$1{,}400$~lines remaining):
  Alladi's formula~\cite{Alladi1977} for the density of
  $\{m : \minFac(m) = p\}$ combined with CRT sieving gives
  PrimesEquidistInAP~$\Rightarrow$~MFRE\@.
  The reduction scaffolding is now formalized in
  \code{Population/AlladiDensity.lean} (267~lines, zero \code{sorry}),
  which decomposes the chain into two precisely stated
  open hypotheses:
  \defname{PrimesEquidistImpliesRoughLPF}
  (Alladi density + weight independence,
  ${\sim}600$--$800$~lines) and
  \defname{RoughLPFImpliesMFRE}
  (CRT sieve transfer from generic $q$-rough integers to
  shifted squarefree integers, ${\sim}400$--$600$~lines).
  The master reduction
  \lean{EM/Population/AlladiDensity.lean}{wpnt\_dsl\_implies\_mc}
  composes these with the proved ANT chain and DSL
  to give MC\@.
  The sieve density infrastructure already exists
  (\code{Reduction/ShiftedDensity.lean}).
  The key observation is that the Alladi weight
  $h(p) = (1/p) \cdot \prod_{q < r < p}(1 - 1/r)$
  depends on~$p$ only through~$|p|$,
  not through $p \bmod q$, so weighted Dirichlet equidistribution
  transfers to minFac residues.
  No prior formalization of Alladi's density formula or
  the Buchstab identity exists in any proof assistant.
\end{enumerate}

\noindent
We estimate ${\sim}1{,}300$--$2{,}300$ lines to close the full
chain.  The Abel summation step is complete
(\code{Population/AbelChain.lean}), the Alladi chain scaffolding with
master reduction is formalized (\code{Population/AlladiDensity.lean}),
and the Wiener--Ikehara bridge and Alladi density content
are the two remaining components.
Formalizing ANT is feasible future work but does not affect
the paper's contribution, since the reduction DSL~$\Rightarrow$~MC
is the novel content.
