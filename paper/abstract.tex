\begin{abstract}
The Euclid--Mullin sequence is defined by $a(0)=2$,
$a(n{+}1) = \text{lpf}(a(0)\cdots a(n)+1)$, where $\text{lpf}$ is
the least prime factor.
Mullin's Conjecture (MC, 1963) asserts that every prime eventually
appears.  We present a Lean~4 formalization
(${\sim}47{,}900$~lines, 84~files, zero~\texttt{sorry}) that reduces MC to a
single open hypothesis.

The main result is the machine-verified chain
\[
  \mathrm{DSL}
  \;\Longrightarrow\;
  \mathrm{CME}
  \;\Longrightarrow\;
  \mathrm{CCSB}
  \;\Longrightarrow\;
  \mathrm{MC},
\]
where \textbf{Conditional Multiplier Equidistribution}~(CME) states
that the $\minFac$ operation destroys correlation between consecutive
multiplier residues mod~$q$, and the
\textbf{Deterministic Stability Lemma}~(DSL) asserts that
population-level equidistribution of least prime factors---a standard
consequence of analytic number theory---transfers to conditional
equidistribution for the specific EM orbit.  DSL is the sole remaining
hypothesis: it is the statement $\mathrm{PE} \Rightarrow
\mathrm{CME}$, where PE is the population equidistribution that
follows from Alladi's theorem on least prime factors.

The reduction proceeds in stages.  An \emph{inductive bootstrap}
recasts MC as a walk-hitting problem on $(\ZZ/q\ZZ)^\times$ and shows
the algebraic precondition (SubgroupEscape) is free.  A
\emph{character-analytic bridge} proves CME $\Rightarrow$ CCSB for
all character orders, bypassing the $d \geq 3$ barrier.  An
\emph{ensemble averaging} framework, parametrizing the EM construction
by squarefree starting points and using CRT decorrelation, collapses
the full landscape to DSL\@.
An independent route through \emph{Visit Equidistribution}~(VE)---the
walk visits every residue class with equal frequency---also implies MC
via a separate machine-verified chain, bypassing CME entirely.

Over 129~dead ends are
documented, trying to delineate the boundary of current methods as precisely as possible.
