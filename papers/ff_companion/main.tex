\begin{abstract}
We study the function field analog of the Euclid--Mullin sequence over
$\Fpt$, where monic irreducible polynomials play the role of primes.
Our main result is an unconditional almost-all theorem: for density-one
monic squarefree starting polynomials, the greedy factorization walk
reaches every monic irreducible target.
The proof proceeds via a six-step analytic number theory chain---character
sum cancellation, a prime polynomial theorem in arithmetic progressions,
forbidden class divergence, sieve product vanishing, confined density decay,
and pigeonhole capture---each step of which is \emph{unconditional} over
$\Fpt$, in contrast to the integer setting where the chain requires the
Weighted Prime Number Theorem in arithmetic progressions.

We prove that the orbit-specificity barrier---the gap between population-level
equidistribution and orbit-level equidistribution---is the sole remaining
obstacle to the full function field Mullin Conjecture, and that this barrier
is structurally identical to the integer case. Three formally verified dead
ends confirm that the Weil bound, large monodromy, and subgroup escape are
each insufficient to close this gap.

We also prove an unconditional exclusion criterion: for primes $p \equiv 2
\pmod{3}$ with $p \geq 5$, the autonomous map $f(w) = w(w+1)$ on $\Fp$ has
no preimage of $-1$, giving a structural obstruction to degree-one capture
in the function field setting with no integer analog.

The entire development comprises 17 files and 7{,}372 lines of Lean~4 code,
with zero \texttt{sorry} axioms, verified against Mathlib.
\end{abstract}
